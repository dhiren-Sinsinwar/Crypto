How to Buy Bitcoin

A step-by-step guide to buying your first Bitcoin in India — safely, legally, and simply.

⏱ 10 min read🟢 Beginner📅 Updated May 2026

Before You Buy: The Basics

Buying Bitcoin is simpler than it sounds. You don't need to buy a whole Bitcoin — you can buy as little as ₹100 worth. Here's what you'll need to get started:

  • A smartphone or computer
  • A bank account or UPI-linked account
  • Your PAN card (mandatory for KYC)
  • Your Aadhaar card or other ID
⚠️ Important: Only invest money you can afford to lose completely. Bitcoin's price can fall 50%+ in a matter of weeks. Never borrow to invest in crypto.

Step-by-Step: Buying Bitcoin

1

Choose a Reputable Exchange

Pick an exchange registered with India's Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). CoinDCX, WazirX, and ZebPay are popular Indian options. Binance and Kraken work for international exposure.

💡 Indian exchanges make INR deposits via UPI easiest
2

Create Your Account

Sign up with your email address and create a strong, unique password. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) with an authenticator app — this is non-negotiable for security.

🔐 Use Google Authenticator or Authy for 2FA, not SMS
3

Complete KYC Verification

Indian regulations require exchanges to verify your identity. You'll need to upload a photo of your PAN card, a selfie, and sometimes your Aadhaar. This process takes 15 minutes to 48 hours.

📄 KYC is required by law — any exchange not asking is a red flag
4

Deposit Indian Rupees

Add funds to your exchange wallet using UPI, NEFT, or bank transfer. Most Indian exchanges accept UPI instantly. There may be small deposit fees.

💰 Start with ₹1,000–₹5,000 for your first purchase
5

Place Your Buy Order

Search for BTC/INR on the exchange. For beginners, use a market order — it buys immediately at the current price. A limit order lets you set a target price, but it may not fill immediately.

📊 Watch live BTC prices on BookMyCrypto before buying
6

Secure Your Bitcoin

For small amounts, leaving crypto on the exchange is fine. For larger holdings (₹1L+), move your Bitcoin to a hardware wallet (Ledger or Trezor) for maximum security — you control the keys.

🔑 "Not your keys, not your coins" — exchange hacks do happen

Tax Rules in India (2026)

Crypto gains are taxable in India. Here's what you need to know:

30%
Flat tax on crypto profits
1%
TDS on transactions over ₹10,000
₹0
Loss set-off allowed (within same coin)

You must declare crypto income in your ITR under "Income from Virtual Digital Assets." Keep records of every purchase and sale including dates, amounts, and prices.

📋 Consult a CA familiar with crypto taxation before filing. Rules may change annually.

Safety Tips

  • Never share your seed phrase — the 12/24 word recovery phrase for your wallet. No legitimate platform will ever ask for it.
  • Beware of fake apps — always download exchange apps from official app stores, double-checking the developer name.
  • Use unique passwords — your crypto exchange password should not be used anywhere else.
  • Verify URLs — phishing sites mimic real exchanges. Bookmark the real URL.
  • Enable withdrawal whitelist — most exchanges let you restrict withdrawals to pre-approved addresses.
  • Never invest based on WhatsApp/Telegram tips — these are almost always scams or pump-and-dump schemes.
